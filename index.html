Fabergé Eggs

The Fabergé Eggs are a collection of exquisite jeweled eggs originally crafted by Peter Carl Fabergé for the Russian Imperial family. The first egg was commissioned in 1885 by Tsar Alexander III as an Easter gift for his wife, Empress Maria Feodorovna. These eggs became a symbol of luxury, featuring intricate designs and hidden surprises. Today, they are considered priceless artifacts of craftsmanship and artistry.
